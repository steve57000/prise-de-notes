# English Grammar: Tenses

## 1. The Past Tense (Le passé)

### Simple Past (Passé simple)
The simple past is used to describe actions that happened at a specific time in the past.

**Structure:**
Subject + Verb (past form)

**Examples:**
- I played football yesterday. (J'ai joué au football hier.)
- She visited her grandmother last weekend. (Elle a rendu visite à sa grand-mère le week-end dernier.)

### Past Continuous (Passé continu)
The past continuous is used to describe an action that was happening at a specific time in the past.

**Structure:**
Subject + was/were + Verb(-ing)

**Examples:**
- I was reading a book at 8 pm. (Je lisais un livre à 20h.)
- They were watching TV when I arrived. (Ils regardaient la télévision quand je suis arrivé.)

### Past Perfect (Plus-que-parfait)
The past perfect is used to describe an action that was completed before another action in the past.

**Structure:**
Subject + had + Past Participle

**Examples:**
- He had finished his homework before dinner. (Il avait fini ses devoirs avant le dîner.)
- They had left when we arrived. (Ils étaient partis quand nous sommes arrivés.)

## 2. The Present Tense (Le présent)

### Present Simple (Présent simple)
The present simple is used to describe habitual actions, general truths, and fixed events.

**Structure:**
Subject + Verb (base form)  
For third-person singular: Verb + -s/-es

**Examples:**
- She walks to school every day. (Elle marche à l'école tous les jours.)
- The sun rises in the east. (Le soleil se lève à l'est.)

### Present Continuous (Présent continu)
The present continuous is used to describe actions happening right now or around the current time.

**Structure:**
Subject + am/is/are + Verb(-ing)

**Examples:**
- I am studying English. (J'étudie l'anglais.)
- They are playing outside. (Ils jouent dehors.)

### Present Perfect (Passé composé)
The present perfect is used to describe an action that happened at an unspecified time before now or something that started in the past and continues into the present.

**Structure:**
Subject + have/has + Past Participle

**Examples:**
- I have lived here for five years. (J'habite ici depuis cinq ans.)
- She has already seen that movie. (Elle a déjà vu ce film.)

---

## 3. The Future Tense (Le futur)

### Future Simple (Futur simple)
The future simple is used to describe actions that will happen in the future.

**Structure:**
Subject + will + Verb (base form)

**Examples:**
- I will go to the store tomorrow. (J'irai au magasin demain.)
- They will finish the project next week. (Ils termineront le projet la semaine prochaine.)

### Future Continuous (Futur continu)
The future continuous is used to describe an action that will be happening at a specific time in the future.

**Structure:**
Subject + will be + Verb(-ing)

**Examples:**
- This time tomorrow, I will be flying to Paris. (Demain à cette heure, je serai en train de voler vers Paris.)
- She will be working late tonight. (Elle travaillera tard ce soir.)

### Future Perfect (Futur antérieur)
The future perfect is used to describe an action that will be completed before a specific point in the future.

**Structure:**
Subject + will have + Past Participle

**Examples:**
- By next year, I will have graduated from university. (D'ici l'année prochaine, j'aurai terminé mes études universitaires.)
- She will have finished the book by the time we arrive. (Elle aura terminé le livre lorsque nous arriverons.)
